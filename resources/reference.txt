1. Basic Identification & Demographic Information

This includes information for identifying and categorizing the patient:

PatientID (Primary Key)

Full Name (First, Last, Middle)

Date of Birth

Age (calculated from Date of Birth)

Gender (e.g., Male, Female, Non-binary, etc.)

Biological Sex (e.g., Male, Female, etc.)

Blood Type (e.g., A+, B-, O+, AB+, etc.)

Height (in cm or inches)

Weight (in kg or lbs)

Contact Information (e.g., Address, Phone number, Email)

2. Core Medical Information

This includes important medical facts that don’t change over time and help provide insights into the patient’s overall health:

Allergies (Drug, food, environmental, etc.)

Medical Conditions (e.g., chronic conditions like diabetes, asthma, etc.)

Past Surgeries/Procedures (e.g., appendectomy, knee surgery)

Immunization History (e.g., vaccines like flu, MMR, COVID-19)

Genetic Conditions (e.g., sickle cell anemia, cystic fibrosis, etc.)

Family Medical History (e.g., history of heart disease, cancer)

Medications (Current and past medications that the patient is or was taking)

This can be stored in a separate table with a PatientID reference for easy updates.

3. Lifestyle & Social Information

Information that provides context for health decisions and helps with managing treatment plans:

Smoking Status (e.g., Smoker, Non-smoker, Former smoker)

Alcohol Use (e.g., None, Moderate, Heavy)

Drug Use (e.g., Illicit drug use, none)

Dietary Preferences (e.g., Vegetarian, Vegan, Gluten-free)

Physical Activity Level (e.g., Sedentary, Active, Highly active)

Occupation (For context on possible exposures to health risks)

Living Situation (e.g., independent living, assisted living, living alone)

4. Gender-Specific Medical Information

These can vary based on biological sex and are specific to health conditions and treatments related to gender:

Menstrual History (if applicable — age of menarche, menopause status, cycle regularity)

Pregnancy History (number of pregnancies, miscarriages, deliveries)

Prostate Health (for males — history of prostate issues, cancer screenings)

Breast Cancer History (for females — family history, screenings like mammograms)

Gynecological History (for females — history of pap smears, gynecological surgeries)

5. Advanced Care Preferences (Optional, but Important for Critical Care)

This part helps in decision-making for end-of-life care or critical situations:

Do Not Resuscitate (DNR) Orders (if applicable)

Living Will / Advanced Directives (if applicable)

Organ Donation Status (e.g., Yes, No, Unsure)

End-of-Life Care Preferences (e.g., Palliative care, hospice care preferences)

6. Emergency & Contact Information

Essential for emergencies and ensuring communication:

Primary Care Physician (PCP) (Name, contact details)

Emergency Contact(s) (Name, relationship, phone number)

Caregiver Information (Name, relationship, contact details)

Legal Documents (e.g., Power of Attorney for healthcare, legal guardianship)
